Anovate Cream is a combination medicine used for the treatment of piles (hemorrhoids). It relieves the pain, swelling, itching, and discomfort associated with passing of stools in people who suffer this problem in the anal area.

Anovate Cream is a combination of three medicines: Phenylephrine, Beclometasone and Lidocaine, which treats piles. Phenylephrine is a decongestant which shrinks the blood vessels in the affected area of skin and decreases swelling. Beclometasone is a steroid which blocks the production of certain chemical messengers (prostaglandins) that make the skin red, swollen and itchy. Lidocaine is a local anesthetic which works by blocking pain signals from the nerves to the brain thereby decreasing pain sensation.

  • Phenylephrine (0.10% w/w)
  • Beclometasone (0.025% w/w)
  • Lidocaine (2.50% w/w)
  • Reduces inflammation and swelling in affected areas.
  • Provides relief from itching, burning, and pain caused by hemorrhoids.
  • Promotes healing of rectal tissue and reduces irritation.
  • Combines anti-inflammatory, anesthetic, and vasoconstrictor effects for effective symptom relief.
  • Useful for treating external and internal hemorrhoids.
  • Helps reduce the discomfort associated with anal fissures and other anorectal conditions.

Apply ANOVATE CREAM as advised by your physician. It is for rectal use only. Use it at the dose and duration advised by your doctor. Check the label for directions before use. Clean and dry the affected area, apply the medicine, and wash your hands after applying it.

  • If you forgot to apply a dose of ANOVATE CREAM, apply it as soon as you remember. However, if it is time for the next dose of ANOVATE CREAM, skip the missed dose and apply the next one regularly. Do not apply a double dose of ANOVATE CREAM to compensate for the missed ones.
  • Avoid using any other topical medicines along with ANOVATE CREAM without consulting your doctor.
  • ANOVATE CREAM should be used with caution in pregnant and breast-feeding women. Consult with your doctor before using.
